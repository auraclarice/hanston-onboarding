Day 1–2 · After HR Orientation
NetSuite Walkthroughs
NetSuite is Hanston’s system for HR transactions. Select your role to see the walkthroughs that apply to you.
Access first: Confirm your NetSuite account is activated by HR before starting. Email humanresources@hanstonproperties.com if you need access.
👤
Rank & File
Staff · Associate · Coordinator · Technician
👔
Manager / Lead
Supervisor · Manager · Dept Head · Team Lead
Showing walkthroughs for Rank & File employees.

File at least 2 working days in advance for planned leaves.

  1. NetSuite → Employee Center
  2. Click Time Off → Request Time Off
  3. Select leave type (Vacation / Sick / Emergency), dates, and add reason note
  4. Submit — direct lead is notified automatically for approval
  5. Attach medical certificate if SL exceeds 2 consecutive days (submit upon return)
Tip: You can check your leave balance from the same Employee Center page.

OT must be pre-approved by your direct lead before rendering. Never render OT without prior approval.

  1. Get Viber or verbal approval from your direct lead before rendering OT
  2. After rendering: NetSuite → Employee Center → Time → Log Time
  3. Select date, enter hours, Type: Overtime, describe work done
  4. Submit for lead approval
Important: OT filed without prior approval may not be compensated.

For work-related out-of-pocket expenses. Keep your official receipts. File within 5 working days.

  1. NetSuite → Employee Center → Expenses → Enter Expense Report
  2. Enter: date, category, amount, business purpose
  3. Attach scan/photo of your official receipt
  4. Submit → routes to direct lead then Finance. Processed on next payroll cycle.
Reminder: Missing ORs = claim returned. No exceptions.

For requesting company payment to a supplier or service provider.

  1. NetSuite → Transactions → Payment Request
  2. Enter: payee name, amount, purpose, cost center/department
  3. Attach supporting docs: SOA, invoice, delivery receipt, or PO
  4. Submit → manager approval → Finance processing
Note: At least one supporting document is required. Requests without attachments will be returned.
Showing walkthroughs for Managers & Team Leads — all RF processes plus manager-specific functions.

WFH is not an entitlement. Requires business justification and pre-approval.

  1. Get approval from your department head or the President first
  2. NetSuite → Employee Center → Time Off → Work From Home Request
  3. Select dates, add justification (what you will work on remotely)
  4. Submit → routed to department head for approval

Act on approval requests within 1 working day. Delayed approvals hold up your team.

  1. Log in → check Reminders on your dashboard for pending approvals
  2. Go to Home → Reminders → Approvals
  3. Review, then click Approve or Reject (always add a note when rejecting)
  4. Employee is automatically notified of your decision
Best practice: Check your approvals queue every morning.

Only Dept Heads and Managers can file MRFs. Triple-check before submitting — cannot be edited after.

  1. Access: bit.ly/MRFNetsuite (HR will also share the direct link)
  2. Fill in: request date, target deployment date, job title, immediate superior, department, employee type
  3. Add: no. of requirements, reason (Additional / Replacement), tenureship, justification, job details
  4. Triple-check, then submit — triggers: HR review (1–2 days) → President final approval
Note: Manual MRFs will be returned. This online process is mandatory.
📋 Open MRF in NetSuite →
  1. NetSuite → Activities → Calendar
  2. Use Team View to see your direct reports’ schedules and approved leaves
  3. Create calendar events, set reminders, and block time for recurring meetings (e.g. L10s)

About L10 Meetings (EOS)
Process — Entrepreneurial Operating System

The Level 10 (L10) Meeting is a structured 90-minute weekly meeting across all Hanston teams. Fixed agenda: check-ins, scorecard review, headlines, To-Do review, IDS (Identify, Discuss, Solve). The goal: resolve the right issues every single week.

  • Every team member has measurable numbers they report each week
  • Issues are "parked" and solved in a structured format
  • Everyone comes prepared — no surprises
  • Your manager will orient you on your scorecard contribution
🧠 Quick Check — Process & EOS
☀️ Offline Activity
Shadow an L10 Meeting
Your manager will invite you to observe an L10 meeting this week. Come ready to observe and take notes — you're not expected to contribute yet.
  1. Accept the calendar invite for the L10 your manager sends you
  2. Bring a notebook — note down: who speaks, what gets "parked," and how IDS runs
  3. After the meeting, ask your manager: "What's my scorecard number going to be?"
  4. Write down 3 things you observed about how the team works

Days 9–10 — Practicum: First Real Deliverable
Practicum Pillar
You learn by doing. By the end of Week 2, you'll have completed a real piece of work — not a training exercise.
☀️ Offline Activity
30-Day Rock Setting with Your Manager
In EOS, a "Rock" is your most important priority for the quarter. Your first one covers your first 30 days.
  1. Ask your manager: "What's the one thing I could do in 30 days that would make the biggest difference?"
  2. Write it as a SMART goal: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound
  3. Agree on how you'll measure success
  4. Write it down and save it — you'll report on it in your first L10
